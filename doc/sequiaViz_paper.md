## **Introduction**

Clinical tumor diagnostics routinely produce heterogeneous molecular data, yet these datasets are often analyzed in isolation, limiting their interpretive potential crucial for precision oncology. Modern diagnostic workflows generate rich genomic and transcriptomic data from next-generation sequencing technologies, but the comprehensive interpretation of these multi-omics datasets for individual patient diagnosis remains a significant challenge. Integrating diverse molecular information into a cohesive, patient-centric view is essential for informed therapeutic decision-making in clinical oncology.

Existing platforms for exploring and visualizing omics data fall into three categories. First, specialized clinical softwares that focus on single-data type interpretation deliver high-quality results within their respective domains but operate in analytical silos. This fragmentation requires manual synthesis of information across disparate tools, increasing cognitive load and the risk of overlooking important molecular interactions across omics layers that may be critical for clinical decision-making.  
	Second, cohort-based research platforms such as cBioPortal \[ \] and UCSC Xena \[ \] excel at exploring large patient datasets from public repositories and supporting population-level research. While these multi-omics platforms are invaluable for discovery science, they are inadequately suited for detailed single-patient diagnostics required in clinical settings. Their architecture fundamentally prioritizes comparative analyses across cohorts over the iterative, detail-oriented process essential for clinical case interpretation. Additionally, although some platforms offer local installation, many platforms remain exclusively web-based, requiring users to upload sensitive clinical data to external servers. That is incompatible with institutional data protection policies and regulatory requirements.  
	Third, commercial platforms offer end-to-end integration and automated clinical reporting. However, these proprietary systems may present challenges regarding the transparency and limited customization options with substantial licensing costs, making them less accessible for academic centers and collaborative research.  
Current clinical genomics infrastructure thus lacks a solution that combines patient-specific multi-omics integration with secure local deployment and streamlined clinical reporting capabilities. Such a solution must enable seamless navigation between variant annotation, expression context, pathway visualization, and read-level validation within a workflow optimized for comprehensive individual case analysis rather than large-scale population comparison. To address these limitations, we present SequiaViz, an open-source user-friendly web-based platform designed for patient-centric integration, visualization, exploration, and interpretation of multi-omics data in diagnostic oncology. SequiaViz uniquely combines results from somatic and germline variant calling, fusion gene detection, and gene expression profiling within an interactive framework deployable on local infrastructure. The platform integrates embedded genome browsing for real-time validation, dynamic pathway visualization to contextualize molecular alterations within functional networks, and automated clinical report generation. Built on an R Shiny framework \[ \] with Docker containerization \[ \], SequiaViz bridges the gap between multi-omics data generation and clinical interpretation while maintaining data security and compliance with diagnostic standards. 

## **Modules**

## The patient-centric design allows single-patient diagnosis with multi-patient data management. The somatic variant calling, germline variant calling, fusion detection, and expression profiling modules present data results in interactive filterable tables that allow variants and genes to be selected and flagged. All displayed data and associated visualizations can be also downloaded in multiple formats if needed. To ensure continuity in patient case analysis, the application supports saving and reloading the most recent session, allowing users to continue reviewing and interpreting genomic findings without repeating previous steps.

**Upload data:** This module serves as the entry point for the entire application. In a guided two-step wizard UI, users first select a working directory, choose patients, and define the dataset types for analysis. In the second step, the application automatically scans the selected directory, detects relevant files for each patient and dataset, and displays their status for review. Once confirmed, the file paths are stored, enabling all subsequent modules to proceed with the analysis.

**Somatic and Germline Variant Calling:** Within the variant calling modules, users can flag oncogenic and pathogenic variants for closer examination in IGV \[ \]. When both tumor and matched normal BAM/BAI files are available, the viewer can load and display them simultaneously, enabling direct visual comparison of read alignments at variant loci. This facilitates confirmation of variant presence and helps distinguish true positives from sequencing or alignment artefacts. Variants that remain flagged as oncogenic and pathogenic at the end of the review process are automatically included in the final clinical report. The relationships between variants, their associated genes, and the biological pathways in which these genes participate are illustrated by the Sankey diagram. This visualization offers an integrative view linking molecular alterations to functional contexts and complements the tabular data by revealing pathway-level connections. The Variant Allele Frequency (VAF) histogram shows the distribution of allele frequency values with a fitted trend curve overlay, providing an overview of variant prevalence within the dataset. Both visualizations are available exclusively for somatic variant calling.

**Fusion Detection**: Reported fusions can be reviewed directly within the fusion detection module, which provides immediate access to supporting evidence for each event. Expanding a row in the results table reveals the Arriba \[ \] report for the corresponding fusion, including relevant annotations and quality metrics, as well as an IGV snapshot of the fusion breakpoint displayed in a split-view layout with the tumor BAM file and, when available, the chimeric BAM file. This preview enables rapid elimination of obvious false positives before opening the fusion in IGV for full alignment inspection. The table also includes a notes column for recording case-specific observations and a true/false column to track review progress. Only fusions that remain flagged at the end of the review process are incorporated into the final clinical report as true-positive.

**Expression profiling**: This module presents patient expression data in a color-coded table that highlights genes with notable expression changes, supported by volcano plot for quick visual identification of deregulated genes. To support focused analysis, users can upload a custom list of genes with their own pathway classifications. The module enables side-by-side visualization of patient tumor expression against one or more tissue-matched reference tissues. This provides detailed insights into expression changes within specific tissue contexts. Since each tissue type has a distinct expression profile, no single universal reference works for all tumor types. This is particularly valuable in cases with multiple tumor sites, such as primary and metastatic lesions, where the table displays, side by side, the log2FC values between the patient’s tumor tissue and selected reference tissues. The module also accommodates data from classical differential expression analyses, providing flexibility for various research and clinical applications. Users can flag deregulated genes for inclusion in the final clinical report.

**Network graph:** Network visualization using Cytoscape.js \[ \] displays STRING DB \[ \] interactions between patient genes within the context of pathways obtained from KEGG DB \[ \]. Users can manipulate networks by adding or removing nodes, adjust confidence thresholds for interactions, and filter by evidence types. Nodes are colored according to log2 fold-changes of gene expression, with the option to highlight oncogenic and pathogenic variants and fusion partners selected in previous modules to reveal disrupted pathway interactions. In case of tumor–normal comparisons, nodes color represent the log2 fold‑change between patient tumor tissue and matched healthy control.

**IGV:** This module provides an integrated genome browser for direct inspection of selected variants and fusions within their genomic context. A table of bookmarked items, populated from other analysis modules, enables fast navigation to specific loci or breakpoints. Upon selection, the IGV viewer loads the relevant BAM tracks (including tumor/normal or tumor/chimeric pairs when available) and centers the view on the region of interest. The embedded browser supports zooming, panning, and track configuration, allowing users to validate findings and assess read-level evidence without leaving the application.

**Summary and Report:** The summary module provides a comprehensive overview of user-selected oncogenic and pathogenic variants, true-positive fusions, and deregulated genes across all modules. Clinical reports are automatically generated using either predefined template or user-uploaded custom template with keyword-based variable substitution. The reporting system integrates all selected findings into a structured clinical document suitable for diagnostic workflows.   
	The automatically generated report should be regarded as a draft rather than a final clinical document. Due to limited access to some metadata and the challenge of retrieving context-specific textual annotations for variants, the report requires review and completion by a clinician, who can provide the necessary clinical interpretation and additional patient-specific information.

**Discussion**

We present SequiaViz, an open-source R Shiny platform that uniquely combines patient-centric multi-omics integration with secure local deployment for clinical oncology diagnostics. Unlike cohort-based research platforms designed for population-level analyses, SequiaViz is specifically architected for single-patient diagnostic workflows, integrating somatic and germline variants, fusion events, and expression profiles within a unified interface. The platform facilitates clinical interpretation through embedded genome browsing for real-time validation, dynamic pathway visualization contextualizing molecular alterations within functional networks, and automated clinical report generation with session management. SequiaViz addresses the critical gap between multi-omics data generation and actionable clinical insights while ensuring data security, analytical transparency and accessibility .
